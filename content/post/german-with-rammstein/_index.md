German is hard. Also, I'm lazy.

Let's take a different tack. Rammstein. Sure, it's not everyday German. But we'll learn a few words while we enjoy ourselves.

Want to learn German with Rammstein?

1. Start by watching Rammstein's 2005 concert in Nimes, France. It's one of their best shows ever. Watch the whole thing straight through, minus the penultimate song as it's a Depeche Mode cover, and in English. You can search for the concert on YouTube, but most are taken down for copyright violations. The same concert was published as a DVD under the title [Vökerball, in 2006, and is available for $36 from the official Rammstein website](https://shop.rammstein.de/en/catalog/visuals-sounds/dvd-blu-ray/rammstein-volkerball-dvd-special-edition.html).

<br>

2. Now we're feeling it for Rammstein, let's go deeper. Read their history, both on [their official site](https://www.rammsteinworld.com/en/rammstein/history), and on [Wikipedia](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Rammstein). Search around for interviews. I liked [this one](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=TpMqLqEMdtI) with Richard Kruspe, their lead guitarist.

<br>

3. We'll try to learn every song in the set - one by one over the next few weeks.

<br>

- We'll start with the first song in the list, [Reise Reise]({{< ref "/post/german-with-rammstein/reise-reise.md" >}}).

<br>

- We'll download the song and use [the concert set spotify playlist](https://open.spotify.com/playlist/16W9Rnd9uVxJMbHzrJC4ol?si=a85fe4717cb541ff).

<br>

- We'll keep listening to Reise Reise until we go mad. Over and over. Walk the dog with it. Workout with it.

<br>

- When we're ready to move to the second song we'll just rinse and repeat. Eventually we'll pick up a few new words and expressions, even if they're a little uncommon, poetic, or whatever. But it'll be fun no matter.

Ok! Viel Glück!
